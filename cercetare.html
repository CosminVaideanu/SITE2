Investigating natural and anthropogenic variability through climate modes

University of Bucharest,Faculty of Physics, Romania

The concept of „climate mode” has evolved in order to cope with the complexity of the climate system. A mode of climate variability can be viewed as a set of physical processes characterized by coherent large-scale variations. Having the potential to group and resolve systematic problems in the climate system, the climate modes represent a modern and useful tool in weather and climatology research alike. Current investigations :

Identifying the global signatures (footprints) of known climate modes of internal viaribility in total, high, medium and low cloud cover fields.

Through their influence on both, the water balance of the atmosphere and on the earth’s radiation budget, clouds have the potential to generate a wide variety of feedbacks on the climate system making the the clouds response to a warming climate remains largest source of uncertainty in most General Circulation Models

Clouds stimulate the human spirit

Identifying links between climate modes and cloud formation or evolution could contribute to a better understanding of the physical processes governing the clouds formation and dynamics and to an improved representation of the latter in climate models.

Separating anthropogenic from solar forcing influence on internal climate modes.

Investigating the physical mechanism of interaction between internal climate modes and the Antcarctic sea ice cover.

Cosmin Vaideanu 2016
